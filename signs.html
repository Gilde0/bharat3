Is Someone You Know at Risk?

Gambling addiction often develops in silence. Here are some common red flags to watch for in yourself or others:

  • Lying about time or money spent gambling
  • Borrowing money or selling items to fund gambling
  • Neglecting responsibilities at work or home
  • Becoming irritable or secretive when asked about gambling
  • Chasing losses — trying to win back money after losing
  • Gambling to escape problems, boredom, or stress
  • Feeling anxious or restless when trying to stop

If you or someone you care about is showing these signs, help is available — and recovery is possible.
