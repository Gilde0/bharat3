Why Gambling Is Dangerous

While gambling may appear to be a harmless form of entertainment, it carries serious risks that can lead to devastating consequences. Here’s why it’s so dangerous:

1. Addiction

Gambling addiction, also known as gambling disorder, is a recognized mental health condition. It causes people to keep betting even when it harms their relationships, careers, and finances.

2. Financial Devastation

Many gamblers lose their savings, borrow money they can't repay, or fall into debt. Problem gamblers are more likely to declare bankruptcy than the general population.

3. Mental Health Risks

Gambling addiction is closely linked to depression, anxiety, and suicide. According to studies, problem gamblers have a suicide attempt rate 15 times higher than average.

4. Impact on Families

Gambling problems often lead to broken relationships, divorce, and emotional trauma for children and spouses.

5. Youth and Online Gambling

With the rise of mobile apps and online casinos, younger people are being exposed earlier — often without fully understanding the risks.
